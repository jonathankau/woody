# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Woody is for in-person friend groups playing a one-phone party word game inspired by 谁是卧底. The primary host is likely setting up the game on a phone, passing it around for private word reveal, then using the same phone to advance rounds.

The target audience is American, culturally fluent, early-adult to mid-adult players, with an Asian American tilt but not a niche-only experience.

## Product Purpose

Woody makes it easy to run a full game without a moderator, paper slips, or rule lookup. Success means a host can configure players and rules quickly, the phone can be passed privately without leaks, and the word prompts feel varied, fair, and socially playable.

## Brand Personality

Direct, social, and sharp. The app should feel like a useful party tool, not a marketing site or a toy interface.

## Anti-references

Avoid a heavy board-game rules portal, a generic party-game clone, or an overbuilt voting product. Do not make the host count every ballot in the app when the group can simply vote out loud and enter the result.

## Design Principles

- Keep hosting fast: common actions should be obvious under party-room pressure.
- Protect private information: reveal screens should make phone-passing safe.
- Make rules understandable: explain variations clearly without forcing everyone to read.
- Treat prompts as the core asset: each pack should feel broad enough to replay.
- Prefer familiar controls: standard product affordances beat novelty.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast, touch targets that work one-handed on phones, reduced-motion compatibility, and copy that avoids stereotyping players or cultures.
